About Me

Image by Cailin Stone

Haden Macdonald

Hi! My name is Haden, with no “y.” My last name is Macdonald, spelled “M A C,” and with a lowercase “d.” I was born in Encinitas, California almost 19 years ago. I have two younger sisters, one of whom is in high school and one of whom is in elementary school. As a child, I enjoyed playing with Hot Wheels, riding my bike, and striking out in Little League. I started playing the piano when I was eight years old and haven’t stopped since. In high school I joined the cross country and track teams. I made it onto varsity and ended my final track season with a mile time of 4:42.

When I began the process of applying to college, I didn’t really have any idea what I was doing. I just followed what everyone else did and applied to the state schools, a couple of reaches, and some colleges that ranked highly for political science. And yet, there was no single college that I was excited to go to. Of all the schools I toured, none really stood out to me. Even if I liked the campus, I would convince myself that there was some other reason I shouldn’t go there. I have so many regrets about how I thought about this process and my ultimate decision weighs on me every day. I feel like I missed out on so many great experiences and friends because I chose a college that had a higher ranking rather than choosing a college that I would be happy to go to. That’s how I ended up at Rutgers, the State University of New Jersey. The campus is ugly, overly large, and worst of all, on the East Coast. The dorms were cramped, old, and smelly. An outdated, never-on-time bus system provides the only transportation to get across the sprawling campus. But at least it had a pretty good political science program. I would ask myself over and over again, “What am I doing here?” I felt so lost and directionless. I knew I had to try to make the most of it. I met people, I joined clubs, I went to every class. Then one morning it all fell apart. I couldn’t bear to be at Rutgers anymore. I was bored and miserable. I began the process of transferring.

After a long, arduous process, I made it here. I love the campus at University of Oregon. This university outclasses Rutgers in every possible way. I’m definitely a different person than the one I was six months ago. I am very excited to be here and I hope to meet as many new people as I can.
